Breaking the Stigma



Analysis: The video above addresses the issue of stigma of mental health. The video highlights the fact that there is a lot of fear and uncertainty around mental health as well as a separation between mental health and physical health, as if our bodies and our minds were separate.

        Historically, our society created an environment whereby speaking about mental health issues could get someone put away in what was known as an asylum. Sharing one’s mental health “problems” would make them vulnerable to their family, friends and to authorities. There is a perception that the person facing a mental health problem or crisis was a danger to either themselves or to others. Thus, there is no room for an individual to simultaneously occupy the space of the normal and face mental health problems. As the video highlights, this creates a silence so that people that could otherwise be allies become too afraid to befriend the person with the mental health issue. It is this very silence that I try to critique in my Diary by showing that, despite initial stigma, a happy end can occur by breaking the social barriers that society has placed between the “normal” and “abnormal”.

I wanted to be his friend so he isn’t so lonely all the time, but mom said no. She said that he might bite me or something because he’s crazy. I don’t know though, to me he seems okay. I think tomorrow I’m gonna sit beside him at lunch!

He has this insane Yu-Gi-Oh card collection and let me have some of his cards. He even gave me blue eyes white dragon!! Tomorrow I’m gonna bring my cards so we can battle.

I asked him about his tantrums and he told me he has intermittent explosive disorder. Basically he told me that sometimes he gets really mad, like Goku when he goes super Saiyan, so he throws tantrums. I told him that maybe one day he can tame it and be like mastered super Saiyan Goku! He laughed and said maybe
