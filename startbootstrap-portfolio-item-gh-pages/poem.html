Poem



Description: Within the "Naruto" universe, the villain named "Pain" subscribes to the ideology that "true peace" can only be achieved if everyone understands each other via sharing the same pain. Using this ideology as a motivation, Pain attacks the leaf village (which is the home of the main character), as in the past, the leaf village attacked his home village. Of course, the idea that “if everyone experiences the same tragedy, then everyone will understand each other” is highly controversial because it depends on everybody somehow feeling pain in the exact same way. I attempt critique this universal rationalization of pain in the poem below.



The Pain Poem


Two souls asunder, naturally unique.
Different bodies, different minds.
Their children lost, their hearts intertwine.
Connected by pain, they lament.
But their aches are different, do they not understand?


Both created unique, from a drop of sperm.
Birthed by different parents, raised on different terms.
Complex innerworkings, even twins aren’t the same.
So how could two people ever experience the same pain?
Problematic presumptions, murder in vain.


Love breeding sacrifice, sacrifice leading to hate.
But what bred oppression? Murder dealt in-vain.
Love into hate, life unto death.
The murderer excused? No. Love isn’t to blame.
Dangerous ideology, flaws lit ablaze.


Love breeds hate, I refuse this rational.
Because suffering is preventable.
Pain is circumventable.
The notion that anguish is inventible?
